DATA ANALYSIS

Product Affinity

🔑 The results are disappointing. First, we have very few multi-category orders (785 to be exact). Second, most category pairings are just due to random chance (lift values < 1). This means either the customer came to the site with a set list of products to buy or our site fails to offer the customer a helpful shopping experience.

⭐ Cluster customers by their shopping behavior. Even if they only buy from one category at a time, what types of categories do they browse before checkout? Segment and tailor content based on those signals.

⭐ Introduce “Complete the Set” Bundles: For example, prompt customers buying from bed_bath_table to add related home_comfort items (pillows, throws, lamps) before checkout.

❗ Home decor products are a strong suit. When customers are buying one category of home decor, they are probably going to buy another category related to home decor. We can begin our application of recommendation engines here and see how it fares.

The Stats:
  • At a glance, a Lift of 13.38 is strong, but with only 2 occurrences, it does not yet provide strong enough evidence to say this is actionable
  • Customers who are buying bed_bath_table products are 15% more likely to add home_comfort products to their cart(Support = 0.043, Lift = 1.15)
