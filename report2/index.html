KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS

I was asked to analyze key areas of our e-commerce performance to help different teams make informed decisions. Below are the KPIs I explored

Product Affinity

Identify which product categories are frequently purchased together to inform cross-sell strategies and enhance the recommendation engine.

Product Reviews

Analyze review volume and average ratings to determine which products and categories are resonating with customers, and where improvements may be needed.

Customer Retention

Measure repeat purchase behavior and time between orders to help assess the effectiveness of retention campaigns.

Sales by Location

Examine geographic performance trends to support regional ad targeting strategies.

Logistics Analysis

Analyze delivery timelines and shipping performance to uncover bottlenecks and fulfillment efficiency.

DATA ANALYSIS

Product Affinity

Revealing the customer's shopping list

    Shoppers Aren't Branching Out!

    🔑 Cart variety is surprisingly limited. While customers do buy from multiple categories, nearly all stop at just two. That signals a narrow shopping mission, or missed cross-sell opportunities.

    ❓ Could the site's navigation, filtering, or category layout be preventing deeper category engagement?

    ⭐ Enrich the online shopping experience. Highlight seasonal bundles and showcase trending purchases right on the home page.

    ⭐ Leverage existing behavior: when customers do buy across categories, build on it! Use stronger recommendation language like “People who bought this also added…” to encourage smarter upsells.

    ❗ Customers are browsing fast and buying narrowly. A more personalized shopping journey could keep them engaged longer and increase variety in their carts.

    The Stats:
  • 785 of 99,439 orders were multi-category purchases
  • No order included more than 3 distinct categories (range: 2-3)
  • Only 2.3% of multi-category purchases included 3 different categories

    • Intra-Category Diversity

    ⛅ The bright side: while most shoppers stick to a single category per order, they often explore within that category; purchasing multiple distinct products in one go.

    ⭐ Use category-specific promotions (e.g., "Get 3 for the price of 2" in top-performing categories).

    ❗ Depth over breadth. This behavior allows us to curate discounts, upsells, and bundles around intra-category needs.

    The Stats:
  • 2,541 orders included multiple different products within just one category
  • Food, drinks, and construction tools showed the highest intra-category variety; averaging nearly 3 unique products per order
  • These top 4 categories account for 9.8% of all intra-category variety (out of 51 total categories)
