KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS

I was asked to analyze key areas of our e-commerce performance to help different teams make informed decisions. Below are the KPIs I explored

Product Affinity

Identify which product categories are frequently purchased together to inform cross-sell strategies and enhance the recommendation engine.

Product Reviews

Analyze review volume and average ratings to determine which products and categories are resonating with customers, and where improvements may be needed.

Customer Retention

Measure repeat purchase behavior and time between orders to help assess the effectiveness of retention campaigns.

Sales by Location

Examine geographic performance trends to support regional ad targeting strategies.

Logistics Analysis

Analyze delivery timelines and shipping performance to uncover bottlenecks and fulfillment efficiency.

DATA ANALYSIS

Product Affinity

Revealing the customer's shopping list

    Shoppers Aren't Branching Out!

    🔑 Cart variety is surprisingly limited. While customers do buy from multiple categories, nearly all stop at just two.

    ❓ Could the site's navigation, filtering, or category layout be preventing deeper category engagement?

    ⭐ Enrich the online shopping experience. Highlight seasonal bundles and showcase trending purchases right on the home page.

    ⭐ Leverage existing behavior: when customers do buy across categories, build on it! Use stronger recommendation language like “People who bought this also added…” to encourage smarter upsells.

    ❗ Customers are browsing fast and buying narrowly. A more personalized shopping journey could keep them engaged longer and increase variety in their carts.

    The Stats:
  • 785 of 99,439 orders were multi-category purchases
  • No order included more than 3 distinct categories (range: 2-3)
  • Only 2.3% of multi-category purchases included 3 different categories

    • Intra-Category Diversity is Present

    🔑 The bright side: while most shoppers stick to a single category per order, they often explore within that category; purchasing multiple distinct products in one go.

    ⭐ Use category-specific promotions (e.g., "Get 3 for the price of 2" in top-performing categories).

    ❗ Depth over breadth. This behavior allows us to curate discounts, upsells, and bundles around intra-category needs.

    The Stats:
  • 2,541 orders included multiple different products within just one category
  • Food, drinks, and construction tools showed the highest intra-category variety; averaging nearly 3 unique products per order
  • These top 4 categories account for 9.8% of all intra-category variety

    • Popular Pairings, Weak Affinity

    🔑 While these product pairings top the list in terms of sheer volume, most of these high-frequency pairings occur simply because the categories are popular and not because they’re commonly bought together.

    ⭐ Improve our cross-sell tactics. Low support shows most pairings are random. Avoid relying on popularity alone. Focus on meaningful associations.

    ⭐ Personalize recommendations. Advanced algorithms can use customer-level data to create smarter product suggestions.

    🔜 Next, we will look at the product pairings (greater than 1 purchase) with the highest Lift values!

    The Stats:
  • Only the combination of bed_bath_table and home_comfort shows a true positive association (Lift = 1.146). This means shoppers are 14.6% more likely to buy these two categories together than we’d expect by chance
  • The top pairings (bed_bath_table & furniture_decor) share only 0.07% of all transactions in this data. Rare, rare, rare!

    • Revamp the Recommendation Engine

    🔑 The results are disappointing. First, we have very few multi-category orders (785 to be exact). Second, most category pairings are just due to random chance (lift values < 1). This means either the customer came to the site with a set list of products to buy or our site fails to offer the customer a helpful shopping experience.

    ⭐ Aggregate user behavior into Affinity Profiles. Cluster customers by their shopping behavior. Even if they only buy from one category at a time, what types of categories do they browse before checkout? Segment and tailor content based on those signals.

    ⭐ Introduce “Complete the Set” Bundles: For example, prompt customers buying from bed_bath_table to add related home_comfort items (pillows, throws, lamps) before checkout.

    The Stats:
  • At a glance, a Lift of 13.38 is strong, but with only 2 occurrences, it does not yet provide strong enough evidence to say this is actionable
  • Customers who are buying bed_bath_table products are 15% more likely to add home_comfort products to their cart(Support = 0.043, Lift = 1.15)
