National Conference on Undergraduate Research

April 2024 - Long Beach, CA

This study analyzed how emotion regulation strategies influence mood in Autistic and Socially Anxious adults. Over nine months, participants provided daily self-reports on mood (0–100) and strategy use, generating a dataset with 15,000+ observations. Data was preprocessed in R using tidyverse, including releveling categorical variables, standardizing, merging datasets, and string cleaning with gsub(). A mixed-effects model was applied using lme4 to account for the nested structure and repeated measures. All visualizations were created with ggplot2.
This project reinforced the importance of rigorous data cleaning to ensure accurate analysis and insights.

Utah Conference of Undergraduate Research

February 2025 - Cedar City, UT

This study explored how motivation and effort influence positive emotions in Autistic and neurotypical adults. We hypothesized that these relationships would be weaker in Autistic individuals. While Autistic adults reported lower average positive emotions, both groups showed similar day-to-day increases in positive emotions with greater motivation and effort.
Data preprocessing included renaming and filtering columns, reclassifying variables, and creating within-person variables. A mixed-effects model was implemented using lme4 in R, with complex functions extracting coefficient data for table generation. Predictor effects were visualized using flexplot.

Society of Pediatric Psychology Annual Conference

April 2025 - Phoenix, AZ

This study examined the relationship between HbA1c, time since diagnosis, and BMI on working memory in emerging adults with Type 1 Diabetes (T1D). We analyzed data from 35 participants (ages 18–25, M = 21.6), who self-reported their two most recent HbA1c values, time since diagnosis, and BMI. HbA1c and BMI were standardized, while time since diagnosis was mean-centered (min = 2 years). Working memory was assessed using NIH Toolbox List Sorting percentile scores. A multiple regression model was applied to evaluate associations. To visualize the unique contributions of each predictor, I leveraged ggplot2 in R to generate partial regression plots, effectively isolating covariate effects on working memory.
This project served as my undergraduate capstone at BYU.
